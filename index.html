My Favorite City: Hong Kong

East meets West: neon harbours, green peaks, Michelin dim sum, and night markets—minutes apart.

Why Hong Kong?

Hong Kong is a vibrant metropolis where East meets West. Known for its iconic skyline, deep natural harbor, and rich cultural heritage, it offers something for everyone—from dazzling city lights to peaceful hiking trails overlooking the sea. I love Hong Kong for its incredible convenience, cultural mix, and endless energy. Despite its fast pace, quiet escapes in nature are just minutes from downtown.

Victoria Harbour

Victoria Harbour is Hong Kong’s most famous landmark. Every night, the skyline comes alive with the Symphony of Lights. Enjoy the view from the Tsim Sha Tsui Promenade or ride a classic Star Ferry across the water.

Learn more about Victoria Harbour

The Peak Trail

The Peak Trail gives you a breathtaking panorama of Hong Kong Island, Victoria Harbour, and Kowloon. Take the historic Peak Tram or hike through lush greenery; the circular walk around the summit is a favorite for sunset views when city lights shimmer below.

Plan your visit to The Peak

Hong Kong’s Michelin-Recognized Restaurants

Hong Kong is a paradise for food lovers, with everything from humble cha chaan tengs to world-class fine dining. Tim Ho Wan is famous for affordable Michelin-star dim sum—try the char siu baked buns and har gow. For refined Cantonese cuisine with harbor views, Lung King Heen at the Four Seasons holds three Michelin stars—the first Chinese restaurant in the world to earn that distinction.

Explore Michelin listings In Hong Kong

Temple Street Night Market

Temple Street Night Market captures the city’s lively spirit: fortune tellers, neon glow, souvenirs, and sizzling street food like clay-pot rice and curry fish balls. You may even hear live Cantonese opera drifting through the stalls.

Visit Temple Street Night Market

Quick Facts

  • Population: ~7.5 million
  • Languages: Cantonese and English
  • Currency: Hong Kong Dollar (HKD)
  • Climate: Subtropical—hot, humid summers; mild winters
  • Transport: MTR, trams, buses, minibuses, ferries—fast and frequent
